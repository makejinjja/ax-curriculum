AX Compass Curriculum Knowledge Base
=====================================

## AX Compass Framework Overview

AX Compass is a structured curriculum design methodology that emphasizes:
- Alignment: Learning objectives align to measurable outcomes
- eXperience: Activities are participant-centered and experiential
- Compass: Clear direction with checkpoints throughout the learning journey

Core design principles:
1. Backward Design — start with the desired outcome, then design assessments and activities
2. Active Learning — minimum 60% of session time on activities vs. lecture
3. Spaced Repetition — revisit key concepts across multiple modules
4. Social Learning — peer discussion and collaborative activities for better retention


## Curriculum Template: Onboarding Program (New Employee, 3 hours)

Title: New Employee Onboarding — Company Culture & Processes
Target Audience: New hires, all roles, no prior company knowledge
Total Duration: 180 minutes
Group Size: 10-20 participants
Learning Objectives:
- Describe the company's mission, values, and organizational structure
- Navigate key internal tools and HR systems independently
- Identify their manager, team members, and key cross-functional contacts
- Complete first-week administrative tasks without assistance

Modules:
1. Welcome & Icebreaker (20 min)
   Activities: Name + fun fact introductions, culture values card sort exercise
   Materials: Value cards, name tags

2. Company Mission & Culture Deep Dive (35 min)
   Activities: Leadership video, small group discussion on "what does [value] look like in our work?", gallery walk
   Materials: Slides, printed value statements

3. Organizational Structure & Key Contacts (25 min)
   Activities: Org chart walkthrough, "Who do I call?" scenario exercise
   Materials: Org chart handout, scenario cards

4. Tools & Systems Hands-On (45 min)
   Activities: Guided tour of HRIS, email setup, communication tools; independent practice time
   Materials: Laptop, step-by-step guides

5. First Week Checklist & Q&A (25 min)
   Activities: Checklist review, pair-check ("does your buddy have everything?"), open Q&A
   Materials: Printed checklist

6. Closing & Commitment (10 min)
   Activities: One-word reflection, commitment card (write one thing you'll do this week)
   Materials: Index cards

Assessment: Completion of first-week checklist + manager check-in at day 5


## Curriculum Template: Leadership Communication Skills (Manager, Half-Day)

Title: Communicating for Impact — Manager Communication Essentials
Target Audience: Mid-level managers, 2+ years management experience
Total Duration: 240 minutes
Group Size: 12-16 participants
Learning Objectives:
- Apply the SBI (Situation-Behavior-Impact) feedback model in real scenarios
- Conduct a structured 1:1 conversation using active listening techniques
- Adapt communication style to different team members using DISC basics
- Deliver difficult messages with clarity and empathy

Modules:
1. Communication Self-Assessment (20 min)
   Activities: DISC style quiz, pair sharing of results, pattern identification
   Materials: DISC worksheets

2. The Feedback Conversation: SBI Model (50 min)
   Activities: Model demonstration (video), practice in triads (speaker-receiver-observer rotation), debrief
   Materials: SBI reference cards, scenario cards

3. Active Listening in 1:1s (40 min)
   Activities: Listening inventory self-score, fishbowl 1:1 role-play, group debrief
   Materials: Listening inventory, fishbowl observation guide

4. BREAK (10 min)

5. Delivering Difficult Messages (45 min)
   Activities: Case study analysis in pairs, "what would you say?" practice with coach feedback
   Materials: Case studies, feedback rubric

6. Adapting Your Style (35 min)
   Activities: Team profile mapping exercise, scenario rotation stations
   Materials: Style adaptation matrix

7. Action Planning & Commitment (20 min)
   Activities: Individual action plan (2 goals for next 30 days), accountability partner selection
   Materials: Action plan template

8. Closing Reflection (20 min)
   Activities: Plus-Delta debrief, commitment sharing in pairs
   Materials: Sticky notes

Assessment: 30-day follow-up survey + manager observation checklist


## Curriculum Template: Data Literacy for Non-Technical Teams (90 min)

Title: Understanding Data — Decision Making for Non-Technical Roles
Target Audience: Marketing, HR, Operations staff with no data background
Total Duration: 90 minutes
Group Size: 15-25 participants
Learning Objectives:
- Distinguish between different types of data visualizations and when to use each
- Identify misleading charts and data presentations
- Ask the right questions when presented with data reports
- Apply a 3-step framework to make data-informed decisions

Modules:
1. Why Data Matters (15 min)
   Activities: "Guess the statistic" game, discussion on a recent business decision made from data
   Materials: Slides

2. Reading Charts & Visualizations (25 min)
   Activities: Chart interpretation challenge (6 chart types), pair work identifying what story each tells
   Materials: Printed chart examples

3. Spot the Mislead (20 min)
   Activities: "What's wrong with this chart?" game — groups find flaws in intentionally bad charts
   Materials: Printed bad chart examples

4. The 3-Question Framework (20 min)
   Activities: Framework introduction, apply to a real company report in small groups
   Materials: Framework reference card, sample report

5. Wrap-Up & Application (10 min)
   Activities: Individual reflection: "one data question I'll ask at my next meeting"
   Materials: None

Assessment: Post-session quiz (5 questions) after 1 week


## Curriculum Template: Design Thinking Workshop (Innovation, Full Day)

Title: Design Thinking Fundamentals — From Problem to Prototype
Target Audience: Product teams, innovation leads, cross-functional teams
Total Duration: 480 minutes (8 hours including breaks)
Group Size: 12-24 participants (teams of 4)
Learning Objectives:
- Apply the 5-stage Design Thinking process to a real challenge
- Conduct empathy interviews and synthesize user insights
- Generate and prioritize ideas using structured ideation techniques
- Build and test a low-fidelity prototype with real users

Modules:
1. Introduction & Team Formation (30 min)
   Activities: Design Thinking overview, team formation, challenge brief review
   Materials: Challenge brief, team canvas

2. Empathize: User Interview Practice (60 min)
   Activities: Interview technique instruction, practice interviews in pairs, synthesis of insights
   Materials: Interview guide, empathy map template

3. Define: Problem Framing (45 min)
   Activities: Affinity mapping of interview insights, "How Might We" statement crafting, gallery vote
   Materials: Post-its, HMW cards

4. BREAK (15 min)

5. Ideate: Divergent & Convergent Thinking (60 min)
   Activities: Brain dump (8 ideas in 8 min), crazy 8s, dot voting for top ideas
   Materials: Sketch paper, markers, dot stickers

6. Prototype: Build It (90 min)
   Activities: Paper/foam prototype construction with team roles, build sprint
   Materials: Craft materials, scissors, tape, paper

7. LUNCH BREAK (60 min)

8. Test: User Feedback (60 min)
   Activities: Prototype testing with external users or role-played users, observation notes
   Materials: Observation guide, feedback capture sheet

9. Iterate & Refine (45 min)
   Activities: Synthesize feedback, identify top 3 improvements, update prototype
   Materials: Iteration log

10. Present & Reflect (45 min)
    Activities: 5-minute team presentations, structured peer feedback (2 stars + 1 wish)
    Materials: Presentation guide

11. Closing: Next Steps (30 min)
    Activities: Individual commitment to real-world application, team action items
    Materials: Commitment card

Assessment: Prototype demonstration + peer feedback + facilitator rubric


## AX Compass Design Principles

### Bloom's Taxonomy Verb Guide
- Remember: define, list, recall, name, identify
- Understand: explain, describe, summarize, classify, distinguish
- Apply: use, demonstrate, solve, practice, execute
- Analyze: compare, differentiate, examine, break down, question
- Evaluate: judge, assess, critique, prioritize, justify
- Create: design, build, compose, construct, plan, develop

### Module Timing Guidelines
- Micro-learning module: 5-15 minutes (single concept, one activity)
- Standard module: 20-45 minutes (concept + practice + debrief)
- Deep-dive module: 45-90 minutes (complex skill with multiple activities)
- Never exceed 90 minutes without a break
- Rule of thumb: 40% instruction, 60% application and practice

### Group Size Activity Matrix
- Individual (1): Reflection, self-assessment, journaling
- Pairs (2): Peer feedback, role-play practice, think-pair-share
- Small group (3-5): Case study, project work, discussion
- Large group (10+): Gallery walk, fishbowl, panel discussion, presentations
- Whole group (all): Icebreakers, debrief, Q&A

### Common Facilitation Mistakes to Avoid
- Too much lecture: keep slides to 3 minutes maximum per screen
- Vague activities: always specify exact instructions, time, and output
- Missing transitions: add 2-min buffer between modules
- Unclear objectives: objectives should be measurable and participant-focused
- No psychological safety: open with icebreaker + ground rules for discussion modules

### Energy Curve Management
- Morning sessions: good for analytical, complex content (high cognitive load)
- Post-lunch: use high-energy activities (movement, games, group work)
- Late afternoon: wrap-up, reflection, commitment activities
- Build in energy checks (quick pulse polls or stand-up stretches every 90 min)
